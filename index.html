What’s the Pattern?

Home in Vancouver → Study in Toronto

According to Statistics Canada, 1.3% to 1.93% of bachelor’s degree graduates of post-secondary institutions in Ontario from 2012 to 2021 were from British Columbia. This group partially represents students whose home is in Vancouver but study in Toronto.

When Are Flights Cheapest or Most Expensive?

Using our manually collected dataset of flights from Vancouver to Toronto, we averaged the lowest and highest ticket prices for each month. This reveals which times of year tend to be the most affordable and which months are more expensive to fly.

Average Lowest and Highest Ticket Prices by Month

Now let’s see the data in weeks.

Here we group all flights by the week of the month they were searched. This helps reveal whether tickets tend to be cheaper at the beginning or end of each month.

Average Lowest and Highest Ticket Prices by Week of the Month

Now let’s see the data in days of the week.

Here we arrange our flights like a calendar, with months as rows and days of the week as columns. Darker cells show days when the average lowest ticket price is higher, while lighter cells indicate cheaper days to fly.

Calendar Heatmap of Average Lowest Ticket Prices by Month and Day of Week

Now let’s see the data in time of the day.

Each point represents a flight we searched, plotted by its departure time and ticket price. This lets us see whether certain times of day tend to be cheaper or more expensive to fly from Vancouver to Toronto.
